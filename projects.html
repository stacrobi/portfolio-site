Projects

Examples of how I translate complexity into learning experiences people can actually use.

This page introduces four featured project directions that reflect the type of Instructional Design work I create.

Project One

Web-Based Training (Articulate 360 - Storyline)

  • Problem: Sales teams needed clearer guidance on which VMware Cloud Foundation use cases to position for different customer scenarios, but product complexity made adoption and message consistency difficult.
  • Solution: Designed an interactive Storyline-based training that translated technical product knowledge into role-relevant sales use cases, decision points, and practical customer conversations.
  • Results: Improved seller confidence, reduced ambiguity around product fit, and helped teams engage prospects with more clarity and stronger use-case alignment.
Review Project

Project Two

Business Etiquette: Phone & Email Training (ILT + Facilitator Guide)

  • Problem: Customer interactions were being shaped by inconsistent communication habits, making it harder for teams to project professionalism, build trust, and deliver a strong customer experience across phone and email channels.
  • Solution: Designed an instructor-led training with a facilitator guide centered on real-world application, using role-play, discussion, and scenario-based practice to strengthen tone, clarity, responsiveness, and communication judgment.
  • Results: Contributed to a 22% improvement in communication quality scores, reduced email rework by 18%, and helped participants apply more consistent customer-service behaviors immediately after training.
Review Project

Project Three

Performance Support Toolkit

  • Problem: Employees completed training, but still struggled to apply what they learned in the flow of work when speed, accuracy, and confidence mattered most.
  • Solution: Developed a performance support toolkit with job aids, quick-reference guides, and reinforcement assets designed for just-in-time use at critical moments on the job.
  • Results: Reduced dependency on retraining, supported faster task execution, and improved day-to-day performance by making support easier to access and use.
Review Project

Project Four

AI-Enabled Learning System

  • Problem: Inconsistent intake practices across teams, poor scope clarity at project kickoff, and frequent rework and delays were making it harder to move learning requests forward efficiently.
  • Solution: Designed a structured intake workflow and built an AI-assisted scoping tool that generated scope summaries, learning recommendations, and a suggested delivery approach based on incoming project information.
  • Results: Reduced ambiguity at intake, improved alignment between stakeholders and learning teams, and accelerated early project planning by making scoping faster, clearer, and more consistent.
